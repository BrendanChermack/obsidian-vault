## Technical Analysis Def:
- Study of past market actions to try and assess future price movements
- capitalize on price movements
- history repeats itself

## What are charts really?
- stocks price plotted over a specific timeframe
- graphically see supply vs demand
	 ![[obsidian-vault/Vaults/Course Notes/Learning stocks/media/Pasted image 20250408084405.png]]
- 5 min, month, etc
- more buys prices go up
- more sellers prices go down

## Why do we need them?
- Analyze patterns 
- predict future price movements
- best time to buy and sell

## Candlestick chart
![[obsidian-vault/Vaults/Course Notes/Learning stocks/media/Pasted image 20250408084730.png]]
- One candlestick tells us a lot of info
	- open price
	- close price
	- low and high of one specific time frame movement
- We can see what has happened in the past and we can see what direction it is heading for in the future
- multiple time frames to visualize trends
- Candlesticks close up
	![[obsidian-vault/Vaults/Course Notes/Learning stocks/media/Pasted image 20250408085050.png]]
	- Bullish candle
		- The price that the candle opened at is lower then the closing price
			- started low ended high
	- Bearish candle
		- the price that the candle opened at is higher then when it closed
			- started high ended low
	- From the wicks we can see either the lowest or highest price
	- In the 3rd candle it closed at the high
		- the wick went low but buyers brough it back up
		- bided up
	- in the 4th candle it opened at the buyers stepped in but did get to the top
	- the 5th candle is a Doji candle
		- opened and closed at the same point
		- sideways movement
			- not a strong enough buyer or seller
	![[obsidian-vault/Vaults/Course Notes/Learning stocks/media/Pasted image 20250408085827.png]]
	![[obsidian-vault/Vaults/Course Notes/Learning stocks/media/Pasted image 20250408085853.png]]
	- Starts and ends on the body while the wick shows the overall movement
